National Commission for Human Development (NCHD) was established by the Government of Pakistan in 2002 as a statutory autonomous federal body, mandated with the role to support and augment human development efforts in Pakistan. NCHD is registered under Ordinance No. XXIX of 2002.
In a landmark move to combat illiteracy in Pakistan, the Secretary of the Ministry of Federal Education and Professional Training (FE&PT), with the support of the National Commission for Human Development (NCHD), has announced the launch of the ‘Each One Teaches One’ (EOTO) initiative under the Prime Minister’s Roshan Pakistan – National Literacy Drive (NLD) program.
A training and orientation session of the newly inducted 15 teams from the province of Punjab and KP was conducted at NCHD Head Office Islamabad. The objective of the session was to train the Field Officers in identification of OOSC in ICT and enrolling them in the schools ( NCHD, BECS, FDE, NEF, Sunbeam and Private Schools etc) . It was part of achieving the objective of Zero OOSC in ICT Campaign 2023.
A seminar on the eve of International Literacy Day was organized at AIOU in collaboration with NCHD, Ministry of FE & PT, JICA and other partner organizations. The Honorable Minister Federal Education Mr.Madad Ali Sindhi graced the occasion as the Chief Guest, the VC AIOU, DG NCHD, and Program Manager JICA also joined him on the stage. The Federal Minister during his speech emphasized upon paving the ways towards eradicating illiteracy and to come up with viable solutions for achieving the goal of 100 percent literate Pakistan
